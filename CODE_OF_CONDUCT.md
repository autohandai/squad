# Autohand Squad Code of Conduct

## Our commitment

We are committed to a welcoming, safe, and constructive community for everyone,
regardless of background, identity, appearance, ability, experience, education,
nationality, religion, or personal characteristics.

## Expected behavior

Community members should:

- communicate with empathy, patience, and respect;
- welcome different viewpoints and levels of experience;
- give specific, constructive feedback and receive it in good faith;
- acknowledge mistakes, repair harm where possible, and learn from them;
- respect privacy, consent, project boundaries, and responsible disclosure; and
- focus discussion on what is best for the project and its community.

Unacceptable behavior includes harassment, threats, discrimination, sexualized
attention, insults, trolling, intimidation, sustained disruption, publishing
someone's private information without permission, or any conduct that would be
unwelcome in a professional community.

## Scope

This policy applies in repository issues, pull requests, reviews, discussions,
community events, and other Autohand Squad spaces. It also applies when someone
is officially representing the project in public.

## Reporting

Report conduct concerns privately to the project maintainer at
[igor@autohand.ai](mailto:igor@autohand.ai) with the subject
`Autohand Squad conduct report`. Do not open a public issue about another
person's conduct.

Include the relevant context and links, but do not gather or share more personal
information than necessary. Reports will be handled as privately as reasonably
possible. Retaliation against someone who reports a concern in good faith is
not permitted.

## Enforcement

Project maintainers are responsible for interpreting and enforcing this policy.
They may edit or remove content, close discussions, reject contributions, issue
a private correction or warning, temporarily restrict participation, or
permanently ban someone when necessary. Responses will consider context,
severity, impact, and patterns of behavior. Maintainers involved in a report
should recuse themselves when another suitable reviewer is available.
